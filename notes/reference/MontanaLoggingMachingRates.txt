                                                                      subsequent diminishing returns, combined with a shifting paradigm in regards to active forest management
                                                                      have had signiﬁcant impacts on the economic and demographic make-up of the industry. One way to address
                                                                      these changes and mitigate the associated challenges of continued viability is through analysis of the factors and
                                                                      constraints impacting routine operational costs. To provide a resource for comparison between commonly-
                                                                      utilized logging equipment, the hourly owning and operating costs of select mechanical, ground-based
                                                                      machines were calculated using the machine rate method from data supplied by western Montana equipment
                                                                      dealers. The results were compared to historical cost data, and reasons for increased logging expense were
                                                                      studied and discussed. Inﬂation-adjusted operating costs for ground-based equipment appear to be 32–86%
                                                                      higher than 20 years ago. Reasons include increases in equipment purchase price, fuel, labor wages and
                                                                      beneﬁts, and repair/maintenance expenses as well as a decrease in the number of operational days per year.
                                                                      Keywords: machine costs; United States; historic cost comparison
